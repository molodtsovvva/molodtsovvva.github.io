Academics

I am currently pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in Physics and Computer Science at Oberlin College. I am a part of Prof. Jason Stalnaker's lab, working on undergraduate atomic physics research focusing on optical magnetometry based on atomic vapor cells. Within my research, I am developing the nuclear spin oscillator as a part of the Global Network of Optical Magnetometers for Exotic physics (GNOME). This network of optical magnetometers is looking for dark matter in the form of large-scale cosmological structures composed of ultra-light axion-like fields. You can find more information regarding the collaboration on the GNOME Website.

I am passionate about sharing knowledge. During the Fall Semester of 2023, I co-authored and co-taught the Experimental College (ExCo) semester-long course "UkraineCo," providing students, faculty, and members of the Oberlin community with a for-credit course focused on Ukrainian history and culture. I facilitated classes for groups ranging from 10 to 15 participants, engaging with readings, art, films, and guest speakers to provide context for Ukrainian history, heritage, and society. This way, I tried to address the issue of underrepresented Ukrainian studies in American college curricula.

"Pedalboard Playground"

Together with Ben Rappoport and Alec Sabol-Pulling, I developed and programmed the "Pedalboard Playground" application for music learners and enthusiasts. The application enables users to construct custom virtual boards of effects pedals, applying diverse audio effects to uploaded sounds. "Pedalboard Playground" facilitates a learning environment, allowing users to gain proficiency in digital pedal usage, laying the groundwork for understanding analog audio effects and getting familiar with the signal processing foundations. It provides a cost-effective sound-editing solution by offering 10 distinct digital audio effects, eliminating the need to purchase expensive analog effect pedals. You can find "Pedalboard Playground" here!

Volunteering

Being born and raised in Kyiv, Ukraine, the unprovoked russian full-scale invasion of my country has upended the lives of many people I care about. For that reason, I focus a lot of my volunteering efforts on war relief. Over the past years, I’ve been active as a fundraiser, demonstration, and charity fair organizer in collaboration with Ukrainisches Koordinationszentrum Dresden in Germany. I was a communicator for Ukrainian refugee children at Elyria Westwood Middle School, acting as a liaison between the kids and the school counselors. I helped organize a benefit Oberlin Conservatory artists concert, raising donations for the “Razom for Ukraine” charity foundation. Additionally, I organized a vigil and a fundraiser on the college campus as a part of the “American Students Stand With Ukraine”↗ campaign, collectively raising over $8,000 in donations allocated for purchasing power generators for Ukrainian hospitals. Over the month of January 2024, I volunteered for the "Razom for Ukraine" non-profit organization, focusing on creating BPMN notation for logistic processes for their further automation, more efficient resource distribution, and transparent responsibility allocation.
